History

CHALKDUST EDUCATION FOUNDATION (CEF) is a non-profit organization incorporated in 2004. CEF was formally organized in January of 2006 to provide education, training and resources to faith-based and other community groups. It offers direct services through the development and promotion of model programs for faith-based teachers, volunteers and parents to assist them in facilitating the growth and development of children, especially those labeled as underachievers enrolled in urban public schools.

Committed to "meeting urban challenges creatively," the need for CEF grew out of events related to formation of the BRIDGE the Gap Network, an interdenominational coalition of traditional churches in greater Cleveland. Brought together initially by a for profit entity to consider ways that the faith community could adopt or adapt programs to assist parents and students in public schools, interest in grant writing emerged among the 25-30 churches represented.

As achievement gaps grew wider and the needs of families became more critical, CEF saw the need to provide services to families through programs offered directly to schools. Our signature program, Black Lemonade Project was designed as an asset-based, skills-building program that empowers parents "to squeeze success out of life's circumstances."

In 2014, we recognized the need to bridge the digital divide. The IndeedWeCode computer camp was created to connect African American high school girls to STEM education. The inaugural camp was held at Cleveland State University from July 6-17, 2015. Our corporate sponsor was AT&T. Instructors were provided by Mel McGee, CEO and Founder of We Can Code It.

The Blueberry Lemonade Project is in its pilot phase. It is an alternative to traditional professional development programs. Its key features include reflective conversations, communication skills diversity training and relationship building.
